Wecome to PON-P3

Genetic variations are investigated in human for many purposes (e.g., to aid in clinical diagnosis). Interpretation of the identified variations can be challenging. PON-P3 predicts the tolerance (pathogenicity) of amino acid substitutions in human (MANE specific). It is a machine learning-based approach and utilizes various features inlcuding amino acid features, Gene Ontology (GO) annotations, evolutionary conservation, and others. PON-P3 estimates the reliability of predictions and groups the variants into pathogenic, neutral and unknown classes. The method is based on a gradient boosting algorithm and it has been trained on a large data set. The method is fast and has high performance. Performance of PON-P3 has been extensively tested and compared with the state-of-the-art methods.

PON-P3 was developed in the groups of Prof. Mauno Vihinen, Protein Structure and Bioinformatics Research group, BMC, Lund University Sweden.
